Shadow

Unity, 2018

This project, was made over a month for a game design class, with some early help from my friend and classmate Bryat Sha, who created the menus and level transition system. I created all of the sprites, textures, and all but one of the sound files (the city ambiance) in this project, as well as the character movement scripts, environment effect scripts, and a couple of simple AIs that patrol, enter an alert state upon seeing the player, and have a timed searching behaviour upon losing track of the player.

Rooftop

Unity, 2018

This project, made over two weeks as a final for an art class, was my first earnest attempt to create an asset set for a game. In addition to the modular pipe and fence kits, I made several reusable and scalable textures for the floors and buildings, and a couple of simple scripts for navigating the scene. Please feel free to walk around the level.
Note: Not all browsers support the shaders used in this project, leading to extreme shiny-ness. Should you experience this, try playing the game on another browser (such as Firefox) or downloading it for a more accurate experience.

Ash

Unity & Charcoal, 2018

This project was made over a week as a final project for my Medium and Message class. Each of the textures was drawn first in charcoal, then scanned into my computer to be used as textures. The piece was meant as an exploration of entropy, the sound and visual design meant to evoke ancientness, decay, and echoes removed from their source. This piece is part of a series with Entropy.
Note: It is also recomended that you play this scene from your machine, as certain shaders in this scene aren't present in the web build.
